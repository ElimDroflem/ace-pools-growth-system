ACE POOLS / SALES SCRIPTS

Conversation scripts

Use the structure naturally. Do not read it word for word.

Former customer

Reintroduction call

“Hi [Name], it’s Alex from Ace Pools. We previously worked on your pool through our construction business. We now provide premium ongoing pool care, with weekly checks and a clear report after every visit. I’m offering former customers a complimentary Pool Review to check safety, equipment condition and running-cost waste. Would [day] or [day] work better?”

QR response

Fast follow-up

“Hi [Name], it’s Alex from Ace Pools. I’ve received your Pool Safety & Running Cost Check. Your result highlighted [risk]. I’d like to verify that properly and show you what needs attention now, what can wait and where you may be wasting money. I can visit on [day] or [day].”

Public signal

First contact

“Hi [Name], I run Ace Pools locally. We help owners keep pools safe, efficient and ready to use, with a report after every visit. We are carrying out a small number of complimentary Pool Reviews in [area]. Is your pool already covered by a regular professional maintenance plan?”

Voicemail

Short message

“Hi, it’s Alex from Ace Pools on 07724 546003. I’m calling about a complimentary Pool Review covering safety, equipment condition and running costs. I’ll send a short follow-up where appropriate. Again, Alex at Ace Pools: 07724 546003.”

Qualification questions

  • Where is the pool and is it indoor or outdoor?
  • How old is it, approximately?
  • Who currently maintains it, and how often?
  • What is the biggest concern: safety, water, reliability, equipment or running cost?
  • Is there any urgent fault or reason not to use the pool?
  • Who needs to approve an annual care plan?
  • When would you like the new arrangement to begin?

Objection responses

“We already have someone.”

“That may be absolutely fine. The review gives you an independent baseline: safety, equipment condition and likely waste. If everything is strong, you receive reassurance. If not, you have clear evidence.”

“It sounds expensive.”

“The plan is designed to prevent expensive failures and reduce avoidable running costs, as well as cover the weekly work. I’ll separate essential care from optional upgrades so you can judge the value clearly.”

“Let me think about it.”

“Of course. Which part do you need to think through: the service, timing or price? I’ll answer that now and agree a specific day to follow up.”

“Just send a price.”

“I can give a range, but the responsible price depends on condition, size, equipment and visit time. The free review prevents you paying for the wrong plan.”
